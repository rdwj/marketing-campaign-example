⏺ Beanie Baby Collection Liquidation Proposal

  The Reality Check

  Let's be honest upfront: the Beanie Baby market crashed hard. What was supposed to fund retirements is now mostly worth $1-10 per piece, with rare exceptions. This proposal focuses on extracting maximum realistic value, not chasing the 1999 dream.

  Strategy Overview

  Phase 1: Triage & Research (Weeks 1-3)

  Identify the ~5% that might actually be worth something:
  - Princess Diana bear (purple, PVC pellets, 1st edition)
  - Tag errors (misspellings, wrong birth dates)
  - Early retirees with 1st/2nd generation tags
  - Authenticated rare variants

  Action: Cross-reference your inventory against current eBay sold listings (not asking prices). This tells you what people actually pay.

  Phase 2: Tiered Listing Strategy (Weeks 4-8)

  Tier 1 - Premium Listings (individually listed)
  - Any piece worth $25+
  - Professional photos, detailed tag descriptions
  - Etsy SEO optimized titles: "Vintage 1997 Beanie Baby [Name] Retired PVC Pellets Mint Tags"

  Tier 2 - Themed Bundles ($15-40 each)
  - "90s Nostalgia Pack" (5-8 common pieces)
  - "Zoo Collection" (animals)
  - "Bears Only Lot"
  - "Complete Your Collection" sets

  Tier 3 - Bulk Lots ($0.50-1 per piece)
  - 20-50 piece lots for resellers or craft projects
  - "Destash" lots appeal to crafters who repurpose them

  Phase 3: Marketing Angle

  Nostalgia sells better than collectibility now. Target:
  - Millennials buying childhood memories
  - Parents wanting to share their 90s with kids
  - Crafters (yes, people gut these for stuffing/fabric)
  - Ironically-inclined Gen Z

  Listing copy tone: Self-aware, slightly humorous about the bubble, but genuine about condition and nostalgia value.

  Phase 4: Pricing & Timeline

  | Month | Focus                   | Pricing Strategy              |
  |-------|-------------------------|-------------------------------|
  | 1-2   | Premium + first bundles | Full asking price             |
  | 3-4   | Aggressive bundling     | 15% off, free shipping offers |
  | 5-6   | Liquidation mode        | 30-50% off, larger bulk lots  |

  Realistic Revenue Expectations

  Without knowing your collection size, rough math:
  - 100 common Beanie Babies → $150-300 realistic
  - 10 moderately desirable pieces → $100-250
  - 1-2 actually rare pieces → $50-500+ (if you're lucky)

  Total realistic range for a "thought it was worth millions" collection: $300-1,500

  Key Success Factors

  1. Photography: Clean white background, show all tags, include ruler for scale
  2. Etsy SEO: "Vintage," "retired," "90s," specific animal names
  3. Shipping: Offer calculated shipping AND a flat-rate bundle option
  4. Timing: List premium items before Valentine's Day/Mother's Day for gift buyers

  ---

  - Rough collection size?
  - Any that you suspect might be legitimately rare?
  - Are you emotionally ready to sell at market prices, or is there a floor below which you'd rather donate?
